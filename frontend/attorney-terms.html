Attorney Network Agreement

Last updated: March 22, 2026

This Attorney Network Agreement ("Agreement") governs your participation in the Verom.ai attorney network. By joining the network, you agree to be bound by these terms in addition to the general Terms of Service.

Why we have these terms: Every requirement below exists to protect you, your clients, and the integrity of the immigration legal profession. A safer marketplace means higher-quality referrals, better client trust, and a stronger reputation for every attorney on the platform.

1. Eligibility Requirements

To participate in the Verom attorney network, you must:

  • Hold an active, unrestricted license to practice law in at least one jurisdiction
  • Be authorized to practice immigration law in the jurisdictions where you accept cases
  • Maintain current professional liability (malpractice) insurance with adequate coverage
  • Have no pending disciplinary actions, suspensions, or disbarments
  • Pass Verom's identity verification process
  • Agree to the Attorney Code of Conduct

2. Verification Process

Verom verifies attorney credentials through a multi-step process:

  1. Bar number verification — We verify your bar number against the relevant bar association database for each jurisdiction where you claim licensure
  2. Disciplinary record check — We check for any disciplinary history, suspensions, or sanctions
  3. Insurance verification — You must provide proof of active malpractice insurance
  4. Identity verification — Government-issued photo ID and live identity confirmation
  5. Manual review — During our launch phase, all attorney applications receive human review

Verification is ongoing. We periodically re-verify bar status and monitor disciplinary databases. You must notify Verom within 48 hours of any change to your bar status, disciplinary proceedings, or insurance coverage.

3. Independent Contractor Status

Attorneys on Verom are independent contractors, not employees. You:

  • Set your own fees for legal services
  • Control your own schedule and work methods
  • Are solely responsible for the legal advice and services you provide
  • Are responsible for your own taxes, insurance, and professional obligations
  • May decline any case referral without penalty

Verom does not direct, supervise, or control your legal work. The attorney-client relationship is between you and your client exclusively.

4. Case Referrals and the Marketplace

Verom connects pre-screened applicants with verified attorneys through our marketplace. Participation in the marketplace is optional.

  • You control your capacity — set the maximum number of new cases you can accept at any time
  • You review case details before accepting — there is no obligation to accept any referral
  • Verom does not guarantee a minimum number of referrals
  • Case matching considers your specializations, jurisdictions, languages, capacity, ratings, and response times

5. Fees and Payments

You set your own fees for all legal services. Verom does not dictate, approve, or cap attorney fees. However:

  • All payments from clients must be processed through Verom's escrow system — no off-platform payments for marketplace cases
  • Verom charges a transparent platform fee on marketplace transactions, disclosed to both parties before engagement
  • Payments are held in escrow and released based on milestones defined per visa type (see Escrow Terms)
  • You receive payouts through Stripe Connect to your verified bank account

6. Response Time Commitments

Once you accept a case through the marketplace:

  • Acknowledge receipt of the case within 24 hours
  • Respond to client messages within 48 hours during business days
  • Provide meaningful case updates at least every 14 days
  • Notify the client and Verom immediately if you cannot continue a case

Repeated failure to meet response time commitments may result in reduced marketplace visibility or suspension from the network.

7. Confidentiality and Data Protection

You must:

  • Maintain attorney-client confidentiality in accordance with applicable rules of professional conduct
  • Handle client data in compliance with GDPR, applicable US privacy laws, and destination country regulations
  • Not use client data obtained through Verom for any purpose outside the attorney-client engagement
  • Report any data breach or unauthorized access to client information within 24 hours

8. Prohibited Conduct

Attorneys in the Verom network must not:

  • Solicit or accept payments outside the Verom platform for marketplace cases
  • Misrepresent credentials, experience, or specializations
  • Accept cases outside their competence or jurisdictional authorization
  • Guarantee specific immigration outcomes to clients
  • Engage in fee-splitting arrangements that violate bar rules
  • Use Verom's tools or data to solicit clients away from the platform
  • Create multiple accounts or manipulate ratings

9. Performance Monitoring

Verom monitors attorney performance to maintain marketplace quality:

  • Performance score — based on response times, filing rates, case outcomes, and client feedback
  • Client satisfaction — reviews and ratings from verified engagements
  • Compliance — adherence to response time commitments and platform policies

Performance data is used to optimize case matching and is partially visible to prospective clients (aggregate ratings and response times).

10. Consequences for Policy Violations

Verom uses graduated consequences for policy violations:

  1. Warning — written notice describing the violation and required corrective action
  2. Payment hold — pending escrow releases paused during investigation
  3. Marketplace suspension — temporary removal from case matching (existing cases continue)
  4. Network removal — permanent removal from the Verom attorney network

The severity of consequences depends on the nature and frequency of violations. Attorneys may appeal through our dispute resolution process.

11. Case Transfer

If you are unable to continue a case, or if you are removed from the network:

  • Client files and documents will be made available to the client and their new attorney
  • Verom will assist in transferring the case to another qualified attorney in the network
  • Escrow funds for incomplete milestones will be returned to the client
  • You must cooperate with the transition, including providing case files and status updates

12. Intellectual Property

Tools, templates, and features provided by Verom are our intellectual property. You may use them in connection with Verom cases but may not copy, redistribute, or use them outside the platform.

13. Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement at any time with 30 days written notice. Upon termination:

  • You must complete all accepted cases or arrange proper transfers
  • Outstanding escrow milestones will be processed according to standard escrow terms
  • Your profile will be deactivated and removed from the marketplace
  • You may request export of your non-client data within 90 days

14. Limitation of Liability

Verom is not liable for the quality, outcome, or legality of legal services provided by attorneys on the platform. Attorneys are solely responsible for their professional obligations and legal work product.
