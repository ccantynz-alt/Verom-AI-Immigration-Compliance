Attorney Code of Conduct

Last updated: March 22, 2026

Our commitment: This Code of Conduct protects the integrity of every attorney on the platform. When clients trust Verom, they trust you. Higher trust means better clients, higher fees, and a stronger practice.

1. Professional Standards

All attorneys on Verom must:

  • Maintain active bar membership in good standing in all claimed jurisdictions
  • Practice only within their authorized jurisdictions and areas of competence
  • Comply with all applicable rules of professional conduct and ethics rules
  • Maintain adequate professional liability insurance
  • Complete continuing legal education requirements as mandated by their jurisdiction(s)

2. Client Communication Standards

Do

  • Respond to new case inquiries within 24 hours
  • Reply to client messages within 48 business hours
  • Provide case updates at least every 14 days
  • Set clear expectations about timelines and process
  • Communicate proactively about delays or issues
  • Use clear, plain language clients can understand

Don't

  • Leave clients without updates for more than 14 days
  • Ignore messages or become unreachable
  • Use unnecessary legal jargon to confuse clients
  • Make promises about timelines you cannot keep
  • Communicate case details through unsecured channels

3. Case Management Standards

  • Accept responsibly — Only accept cases you have the capacity, competence, and time to handle properly
  • File timely — Submit all filings before deadlines. Missed deadlines due to attorney negligence are grounds for suspension
  • Document thoroughly — Maintain complete case files including all correspondence, forms, evidence, and notes
  • Update statuses — Keep case statuses current in the platform. Clients and Verom rely on accurate status information
  • Transfer cleanly — If you cannot continue a case, initiate the transfer process promptly and cooperate fully

4. Financial Integrity

Do

  • Set fees clearly before accepting marketplace cases
  • Process all marketplace payments through Verom's escrow
  • Provide detailed invoices for all services
  • Separate filing fees from attorney fees
  • Refund unearned fees promptly if a case is terminated

Don't

  • Request or accept payments outside the platform for marketplace cases
  • Charge hidden fees not disclosed upfront
  • Bill for services not performed
  • Inflate fees after a case is accepted
  • Commingle client funds with personal funds

5. Honesty and Transparency

  • Never guarantee specific immigration outcomes (approvals, timelines, or results)
  • Accurately represent your experience, specializations, and track record
  • Disclose any conflicts of interest
  • Inform clients of realistic chances and potential risks
  • Do not solicit fake reviews or manipulate your platform ratings

6. Technology and Data

  • Use Verom's tools as intended — do not attempt to circumvent security measures
  • Protect client data in accordance with GDPR and applicable privacy laws
  • Do not export client contact information to solicit them off-platform
  • Report any security vulnerabilities or data breaches immediately
  • Keep your account credentials secure — do not share logins

7. Respect and Professionalism

  • Treat all clients, colleagues, and Verom staff with dignity and respect
  • Do not discriminate based on race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or any other protected characteristic
  • Maintain professional boundaries in all client interactions
  • Respect client autonomy and decision-making

8. Reporting Obligations

You must report the following to Verom within 48 hours:

  • Any change in bar status (suspension, probation, resignation)
  • Any disciplinary complaint or investigation
  • Lapse in malpractice insurance coverage
  • Any malpractice claim related to a Verom case
  • Suspected fraud by a client or another attorney on the platform
  • Any data breach affecting client information

9. Enforcement

Verom enforces this Code through graduated consequences:

  1. Coaching — For minor first-time issues, private guidance and education
  2. Written warning — Formal notice with required corrective action
  3. Payment hold — Pending payouts paused during investigation
  4. Temporary suspension — Removed from marketplace, existing cases continue
  5. Permanent removal — For serious or repeated violations

You may appeal any enforcement action through Verom's dispute resolution process. Appeals are reviewed within 10 business days.

10. Acknowledgment

By participating in the Verom attorney network, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to comply with this Code of Conduct. Violations may result in enforcement actions up to and including permanent removal from the platform.
